about me!

March 23, 2020

what’s up!?

Hi! Thanks for checking out my little corner of the internet.

I’m a 25y/o guy in the US, originally from Wisconsin. I’m currently hanging out in Houston. I went to school at UW-Madison and studied computer science.

I hope you’ll read my posts and respond to them in some way. There’s no point to sharing things if it’s only one way! Reach out via email or whatever if you have any thoughts on what I’m saying, positive or negative. I’m excited for this blog to be a way to both learn from others, as well as a platform for me to teach myself things so that I can regurgitate them for others.

my interests

I like to play around with computers and electronics. I was lucky enough to have an EE as a father and an older brother who didn’t mind his younger brother bothering him, which means I’m lucky enough to say I’ve been programming since the beginning of high-school and setting up servers for video games since middle school.

ham radio

I’m an amateur radio operator with the callsign W9NLS and an amateur extra license class. You can find posts about amateur radio under the tag ham. Lately, I haven’t had much time for radio, unfortunately. My primary mode of operation has been working the UHF/VHF FM cubesats, but I own a two HF radios and, assuming my antenna situation improves soon, I hope to get on 40M and 20M more. My dad’s callsign is KV9M and my brother’s is KC9SXX.

linux

My first foray into Linux was sometime in middle school watching my brother setup an old computer with Fedora to host MadCityHD.com – a now-defunct forum and news site for discussing the digital TV switchover. At the time, I couldn’t have cared less about forums and digital television, but what did grab my attention was the compiz wobbly-windows that we enabled while playing around with it. THAT WAS SO COOL. I was hooked.

Later, I found my own old computers and ran garrysmod servers on them.

The distro that I’ve used the most is Gentoo, at this point. But I’ve been doing this for so long that I can hold my own in any environment. I currently use a MacBook as my main terminal, remoting into my desktop which runs Fedora.

Some strong opinions:

  • SeLinux > Apparmor
  • bash > zsh (muscle memory is a hard thing to fix)
  • amd > nvidia (for linux users, anyway)
  • emacs > vim (but I use vim more than emacs, and wrote this in vim…)
  • openembedded > buildroot
  • systemd > what existed before
  • zfs »»» btrfs
